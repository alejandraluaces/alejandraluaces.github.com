Getting Real

March 26, 2013

Recently, I finished up reading Getting Real by 37signals. When I was first assigned this reading, I was told I'd have a lot to say about it.

But I don't. And here's why.

8th Light is the first software company I have ever worked for. Therefore, I am somewhat of a blank slate. Several of my peers have the experience of working in massive companies, companies with management problems, companies that are not run by the principles outlined in this book.

Luckily, because I am such a "blank slate", agile development seems natural to me. As a freelancer, I worked like this without knowing it had a name. It just seems like the easiest way to operate efficiently and get things done. Talk to the customer often, make changes based on evolving needs while sometimes saying "no," and delivering parts of the project once they are ready.

Getting Real is a book about more than just software development. It is a book that is accessible, easy to read, and simple to understand.

This book illustrates shedding excess and working with small steps rather than large, seemingly unattainable goals. It touches on creativity, and how it seems to flourish the more restrictions there are in place. It emphasizes that it's okay for things to change during development, and that this change should be welcome. These are things that not only apply to software, but everything.
