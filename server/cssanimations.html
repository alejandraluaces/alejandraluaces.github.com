CSS Animations

April 10, 2013

Click the screenshot below to see my project in action!

My last reading was Visual Grammar by Christian Leborg. I was made fun of by my co-apprentice: "The books we have to read are four hundred pages and all words! You get to read picture books!" Indeed, this book did have a lot of whitespace, hardly any actual paragraphs, and very sparse words. I was challenged in a different way--thinking, visualizing, and planning versus writing complex mathematical equations and algorithms. And I'm okay with that.

This project on CSS Animations was by far the hardest for me because, though I was not doing anything too terribly impressive, math in the form of coordinates and speed were added in addition to the usual font selection, positioning, spacing, and color choices I usually make.

I stuck to the red/black/white color scheme of the book for the most part, but added some mint in the footer and the top stripe detail in the very first window. I also made it a point to try to work with "whitespace" in general, since it is not one of my strong suits. I like efficiency and thus, try to fit as much as I can into every little area. I wanted to give each window/cell/div/whatever some breathing room so that the viewer could really concentrate on the focal points which were the animations and transitions.

My favorite part of this project is the last window about "weight". Weight is a theory that I really enjoy experimenting with because it can really affect the balance of a page and the way it's interpreted by the viewer. I took eight circles, made them all different sizes, and made them shoot up the page while simultaneously shrinking, hover for a bit in suspense, and then drop back down, growing to their original size. I wanted to illustrate how size and object placement completely change the way something looks. I approached it this way because I wanted to give the illusion of rocks when the circles are at their base location, and then a constellation when hovering around the top (I even made one of the circles a different color, like in Orion's belt).
